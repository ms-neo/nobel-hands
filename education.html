Education of Children

Poverty has become a sensitive and ever remained issue almost in all developing countries of the world. Education plays a vital role in poverty alleviation. In Pakistan, the demand for education is strong but the public sector has been unable to deliver, due to poor management and corruption. As a result, due to poverty, the illiteracy rate particularly of women is very high in the country. Since 1980, the number of private schools in Pakistan has grown from about 3,000 to about 50,000.

One in five of Pakistan’s poorest families sends their children to private village schools. It is estimated that 1 in 10 children out of school live in Pakistan.

Transportation

There are many families in villages who can’t send their kids to public schools due to lack of transport. Most people have no option but to hire private bus or van companies so their kids coud go to nearby city or town. Poor families can’t afford this extra cost associated with the education of their kids. As a result, a vicious cicrle of poverty and lack of development continues.

A survey showed that about one in five of Pakistan’s poorest families sends their children to private village schools. It is estimated that 1 in 10 children out of school live in Pakistan. It is an alarming number and efforts should be made to help these poor communities. Few charities have set up private schools where running projects offer financial support to schools. But it is still only a drop in the ocean.

Our Goal

It doesn’t take a lot to help poor families and and get their children educated. Education can change their lives, for good. Noblehands.org has joined hands to fight this cycle of poverty and give these kids a better future. We are helping these families to stand on their feet by buying them animals in villages. These animals can provide milk to the family. That milk is used by the family and they can sell extra milk to make some extra money for food, clothing and other expenses.
